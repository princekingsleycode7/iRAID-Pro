Water for Umualanchara: A Solar-Powered Transformation

Fully Funded By Poul Due Jensen / Grundfos Foundation — Denmark

For years, the people of Umualanchara Amizi, in Umuahia South Local Government Area of Abia State, endured daily hardship in search of something most take for granted: clean water. Women and children woke before dawn to trek to a distant seasonal stream — one that ran dry every dry season — returning with water that carried the invisible burden of disease. Waterborne illnesses like diarrhoea, typhoid, and skin infections were common. It was a cycle of struggle that eroded time, health, and dignity.

Today, that story is being rewritten. Through the Grundfos Solar Water Scheme for Sustainable Life Program, executed by the Integrated Rural Aid Foundation (IRAID) and fully funded by the Poul Due Jensen/Grundfos Foundation of Denmark, a fully mechanised solar-powered borehole now stands at the heart of Umualanchara Amizi. Drilled to a depth of approximately 137 metres using mechanised rotary technology, the system is equipped with solar panels, a pumping system, storage tanks, a water filtration treatment plant, CCTV monitoring, solar lighting, riser pipes, and a perimeter fence — delivering clean, purified water around the clock, every day of the year.

"Before this project, our women woke before dawn to fetch water from a distant stream."

Community Member, Umualanchara Amizi

The construction process was thorough and community-centred. It began with professional site mapping and preparation, followed by drilling with soil sampling at regular intervals, then borehole casing and compression to secure the well structure against contamination. Finally, riser pipes were installed and pump testing conducted — the moment the community had long waited for. When the first water flowed, residents gathered in celebration and prayer, expressing what many described as a divine intervention for their community.

Community involvement has been a defining feature of this project. In April 2026, IRAID conducted a three-day Water Project Users and Management Training (April 15–17) at Umuifeze Civic Hall, Umualanchara Amizi. Dozens of women attended sessions on hygiene education and basic maintenance of the solar water scheme. A local Water Management Committee was formed and will receive additional virtual training from the Grundfos Technical Team, ensuring the community is equipped to own and sustain the system long after the project closes.

As of April 2026, the borehole and tank stanchion are fully complete (100%), while water distribution infrastructure, pipe laying for fetching points, and the filtration system installation are each at 50% completion and progressing steadily.

The impact of this project extends far beyond plumbing. Children who once spent hours fetching water can now attend school. Women are no longer exposed to the dangers of pre-dawn treks to distant streams. Families have clean water for drinking, cooking, and daily hygiene. And the community looks ahead with renewed confidence — knowing that the arrival of clean water is also the arrival of new opportunity, investment, and dignity.

IRAID extends sincere gratitude to the Poul Due Jensen/Grundfos Foundation for this life-changing investment, and to the entire community of Umualanchara Amizi for their faith, participation, and resilience. This is what sustainable development looks like — not done for a community, but done with one.
